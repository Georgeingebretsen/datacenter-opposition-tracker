Chapter 1

The acceleration

For a decade, data center opposition was rare. Then something changed.

All community actions, 2018–2026
2026 Through
— cumulative actions

Before 2020, data center opposition barely existed. A handful of zoning fights per year, scattered across the country.

By 2022, the pace had doubled. Counties started passing short-term moratoria as projects multiplied.

2023 was the inflection. ChatGPT was a year old. Hyperscalers announced gigawatt-scale builds. Monthly action counts went vertical.

In 2024, new community actions appeared every week. A local movement became a national one.

Chapter 2

What's at stake

A hyperscale data center doesn't draw power like a factory. It draws power like a city.

The median project in our tracker draws 500 megawatts. The largest, in Beaver County, Utah, is planned for 10 gigawatts — more electricity than the state of Rhode Island uses.

Chapter 6

Does size matter?

You'd expect bigger projects to get bigger opposition. They don't.

Median petition size is nearly flat across project sizes. A 50 MW exurban build draws roughly the same signature count as a 1 GW hyperscale campus. Local stakes — water, traffic, farmland — mobilize people more reliably than scale.

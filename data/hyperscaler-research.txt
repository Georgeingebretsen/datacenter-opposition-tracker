Hyperscaler Tenant Research
===========================
Date: 2026-03-02

For each entry: Jurisdiction, State, Hyperscaler, Confidence Level

1.  DeForest (Village of), WI — Microsoft — likely
    QTS (Blackstone) was the developer. CNBC headlined its article "Microsoft data center rejected
    in Wisconsin village." QTS does not publicly name tenants, but the CNBC framing strongly
    implies Microsoft was the end-user. Project withdrawn Jan 2026 after unanimous board rejection.

2.  Maricopa/Goodyear, AZ — unknown — unknown
    Tract's "Project Range" ($14B) was a speculative land-infrastructure play. Tract develops
    master-planned campuses and sells/leases to hyperscalers; no specific tenant was ever named.
    Project withdrawn May 2024 after local opposition. Tract hoped to attract Amazon, Google,
    or Microsoft but no commitment was made.

3.  Mooresville (Iredell County), NC — unknown — unknown
    Tract's $30B campus on Dale Earnhardt widow's property. Same developer model as #2: Tract
    builds infrastructure and seeks hyperscaler tenants. No tenant was ever disclosed. Project
    withdrawn after community opposition (early 2026).

4.  Farmington, MN — unknown — unknown
    Tract's Farmington Technology Park ($5B, 343 acres). City leaders signed NDA with Tract;
    developer declined to identify end user. Tract's model is to develop then lease to hyperscalers.
    No tenant publicly identified. Residents sued to stop the project.

5.  Chesterton/Burns Harbor, IN — unknown — unknown
    Provident Realty Advisors ($1.3B). Texas-based developer targeting hyperscale tenant but never
    disclosed who. Chesterton application withdrawn; Burns Harbor proposal stalled amid opposition.
    No tenant information found.

6.  Wyandotte County, KS — unknown — rumored
    Red Wolf DCD Properties ($12B, 600MW, six buildings). Developer Aaron Wolofsky of Brooklyn, NY.
    No corporate occupant publicly disclosed. Reporting says it would "likely be one of big-tech
    companies" with Google, Meta, and Microsoft mentioned as typical end-users, but no specific
    name confirmed. Lawsuit has delayed the project.

7.  Bessemer, AL — unknown — unknown
    Logistic Land Investment / TPA Group "Project Marvel" ($14.5B, 1GW). End user hidden by NDA.
    City officials barred from discussing. As of July 2025, no end user had signed on. No
    hyperscaler name has surfaced in any reporting.

8.  Jones County, GA — none (speculative) — confirmed no tenant
    EagleRock Partners "Crooked Creek" ($5-7B). Reporting explicitly states "there is no lessee —
    no Intel or Microsoft — which has told [EagleRock] they want the data center." This was a
    speculative build with no committed tenant. Application withdrawn Oct 2025 after opposition.

9.  Sangamon County, IL — unknown — unknown
    CyrusOne ($500M, 600MW, six buildings). CyrusOne is a major datacenter operator (Dallas-based)
    that leases to hyperscalers. No specific tenant identified for this Talkington Township campus.
    Project still in zoning approval process.

10. Statesville, NC — unknown — rumored
    Compass Datacenters (Brookfield-owned), 333 acres, 500MW. Compass's securitization filings show
    all facilities are "100% leased on long-term contracts to four firms that Moody's describes as
    investment-grade hyperscale tenants." Those four are typically Amazon, Microsoft, Google, and
    Meta. No specific tenant identified for Statesville. Rezoning approved Sept 2025.

11. Augusta, GA — unknown — unknown
    QTS ($2B, Gordon Highway). QTS filed plans under "Cyber Development LLC." Facility would be
    twice the size of Augusta Mall. No tenant publicly identified. QTS is a colocation/hyperscale
    landlord (Blackstone-owned) serving multiple hyperscalers, but the Augusta tenant is undisclosed.

12. Coweta, OK — Amazon — likely
    Beale Infrastructure "Project Atlas" ($800M). Beale's separate "Project Blue" nearby was
    explicitly linked to Amazon/AWS (though withdrawn). For Project Atlas, the end user is described
    as "one of the major U.S. tech giants" and it will be "a cloud server, not an AI server."
    Combined with the Amazon/AWS connection on Beale's other Oklahoma projects, Amazon/AWS is the
    likely tenant. Planning commission rejected, but city council vote pending.

13. Page, AZ — unknown — unknown
    Huntley LLC ($10B, 1GW, 500 acres near Horseshoe Bend). Developer traces to UK-based Hollybrook
    and subsidiary Rooskey. Negotiations with a "household name" were reportedly ongoing, but tenant
    never identified. No hyperscaler name confirmed in any reporting.

14. Clarksville, AR — unknown — rumored
    Serverfarm ($8B, 135 acres, six buildings). Owned by Manulife. Company says it plans to "build
    out data centers that have already been leased to undisclosed AI tenants." The tenant is pre-
    committed but identity is confidential. No specific hyperscaler named.

15. Sioux Falls, SD — unknown — unknown
    Gemini Capital / Gemini Data Center SD LLC ($1.9B, 500MW). Developer is a California-based
    family office (Michael Anvar). No tenant disclosed. Site approved for rezoning. Could serve a
    single hyperscaler or multiple colocation clients. No hyperscaler identified.

16. Franklin (Simpson County), KY — unknown — rumored
    TenKey LandCo ($5B, three buildings on 200 acres). End user described as "one of the largest
    tech companies in the world" but not publicly identified. Developer is suing the county over
    new regulations. No specific hyperscaler named.

17. Cherokee County (Gaffney), SC — unknown — unknown
    Cielo Digital Infrastructure ($2.1B, 300MW, four buildings). Texas-based developer that
    describes itself as an expert in development rather than operations. Did not respond to
    questions about whether it will retain ownership or build on behalf of others. No tenant
    identified.

18. Laramie County, WY — OpenAI (Stargate) — likely
    Crusoe Energy / Tallgrass "Project Jade" ($50B+, 1.8GW scaling to 10GW). Crusoe declined to
    name tenants "but did not deny reports the site could be linked to OpenAI's Stargate project."
    Crusoe is already building a 1.2GW campus for Oracle/OpenAI in Abilene, TX. The scale, AI focus,
    and Crusoe-OpenAI relationship strongly suggest OpenAI/Stargate. Approved Jan 2026.

19. Cameron County (Harlingen), TX — unknown — unknown
    Eneus Energy ($14B, 2GW, 1,785 acres). UK/Scotland-based powered-land developer. No tenant
    disclosed. Project is in "initial planning and evaluation phase" with no formal proposal
    submitted to local authorities as of Feb 2026.

20. San Marcos, TX — CyrusOne (developer, not tenant) — unknown tenant
    Highlander SM One LLC ($1.5B, 200 acres). Dallas-based CyrusOne was "contractually tied to
    the development" but the agreement expired due to delays. Multiple potential operators remain
    interested but none confirmed. City council rejected the zoning change Feb 2026. No end-user
    hyperscaler identified.
